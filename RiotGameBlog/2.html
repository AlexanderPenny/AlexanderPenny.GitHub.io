Figure 1

Health bar implementation, as shown in this screenshot, has been added. This photo is from the demonstration of the protester aggression toward the riot police mechanic. At this moment in time, the health bars do not show if health is full. However, they will show if health is not full, to keep the screen uncluttered while displaying the information where necessary.

Depending on future development, I may find it better to only show health above a certain level when the team is being hovered over. As more riot police are added during development toward a fully playable prototype, this current system may become too visually cluttered, especially with the future implementation of riot police models.

Aggression and Interactions Between Police and Protesters

There are three states of aggression that have been programmed into the game so far, based on a "stress" value.

The protesters can be in the following states, in order:

  • No Stress: No aggression is shown toward riot police.
  • Aggressive: The next step up in stress level, where protesters move toward the police and keep a distance. This is an intermediary stage where it is still peaceful but starting to become aggressive, for whatever reason.
  • Attacking: Protesters will attack the police, as shown in Figure 2.

Figure 2

At this attacking stage, different protesters can be at different stress levels. However, I will likely implement a system, for which I have not fully decided the architecture yet, where the protester AIs may affect each other's stress levels.

Zone Detection System

Figure 3

As seen above, this is the zone layout used for detection.

I opted for this system for reasons of computational efficiency. As this game intends to have many different and individually operating NPCs at once, checking for other NPCs fitting a description every second, or likely more often than that, would be far too computationally intense for more than 700 NPCs.

Instead, a zone system is used, where each zone has a list of the NPCs within it. Each zone, as has been thankfully linked up and drawn by Ilan Frasca, contains knowledge of which zones are next to it. All an NPC needs to do is query the zone it knows it is in, then query the neighbouring zones for the NPCs registered to any of those zones. This forms the NPC's perception.

This also effectively manages NPCs by ensuring that they are not able to see through walls.
